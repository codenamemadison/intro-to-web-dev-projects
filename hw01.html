Madison Shimbo's Reading Reflection

“As We May Think” - Vannevar Bush, 1945

In this article, Vannevar Bush discusses the many interesting ways technology has influenced not only how we preserve information and knowledge but also how we organize, consult, and interact with this information and data. On the same note, Bush discusses the idea of the “memex”, or a device that can be used to “[store] all his books, records, and communications”. This memex would allow the user to more effectively manage and access different types of content as well as to be able to share this content with others. This revolutionary idea would later materialize into what we know as the World Wide Web and the internet.

"Long Live the Web" - Tim Berners Lee, 2010

In this article, Time Berners Lee brings up many interesting concerns as to how the Web should be and how it is being controlled. Lee discusses how the Web’s greatness lies in its importance in a democracy, its ability to host global conversations surrounding issues people care about, and its universality in who can access the content. In these ways does Lee brings up the issue surrounding net neutrality and how the ways the internet and the Web help us can be used to potentially hurt us. Specifically, Lee makes many compelling points surrounding how, without net neutrality, Internet Service Providers (ISP)’s can potentially control how fast or slow your connection is or what type of content you can access and make these decisions based on their own biases. This makes me think about how much power ISP’s have since, without net neutrality, ISP’s could have the power to purposefully limit what kind of content we consume, ranging from which newspapers and news outlets we have access/have a faster connection to, to what type of entertainment you can view online. This made me realize how ISP’s essentially have the potential to completely shape how we learn about the world and our perception of it through what we are able to access on the Internet and how scary this potential is. In doing so, they would be able to shape the thoughts and knowledge of their customers and take advantage of what we have taken for granted.
